Pugs: Best Dog Ever

A Brief History

Pugs originated in China, around 400 B.C. at the earliest, but they're potentially older. Pugs average lifespan is 11-15, with the oldest recorded pug, Snookie, dying in 2018 at the age of 27. Pugs usually experience multiple health problems due to being bread to purposely have shorter snouts. Some of these problems include bad eyesight, or trouble breathing. Pugs are generally pretty chubby for their size, with their typical breed weight being 14-18 pounds but can be as heavy as 30 pounds before being classified as overweight!


Fun Facts!

Pugs are generally regarded as a little ugly, but are actually the 28th most popular dog breed in the U.S. according to the American Kennel Club. Pugs were pets in China in ancient Tibetan Buddhist monasteries. Pugs have a double coat, which means they are heavy shedders and get hair everywhere. According to Purina, pugs are often referred to as "multum in parvo" which is latin for much in little. This is of course talking about pugs big personalites but little size, as pugs are very expressive and are believed to understand their owners moods.
