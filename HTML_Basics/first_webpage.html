A Brief History

Pugs originated in China around 400 B.C. Pugs average lifespan is 11-15. Pugs usually experience multiple health problems due to being bread to purposely have shorter snouts. Some of these problems include bad eyesight, or trouble breathing. Pugs are generally pretty chubby for their size, with their typical breed weight being 14-18 pounds but can be as heavy as 30 pounds before being classified as overweight!


Basic Facts About Pugs

  • Pugs fur can have silver, apricot, fawn, or black fur
  • The oldest pug to ever live was named Snookie, who died in 2018 at the age of 27
  • The pugs average size is 10-12 for females, and 12-14 for males
  • Pugs are heavy breathers and actually snore when asleep
  • Pugs are pretty lazy animals

Fun Facts!

Pugs are generally regarded as a little ugly, but are actually the 28th most popular dog breed in the U.S. according to the American Kennel Club. Still not geat numbers, but it's something, especially considering there's 200 different breeds in the U.S. Pugs were pets in China in ancient Tibetan Buddhist monasteries. Pugs have a double coat, which means they are heavy shedders and get hair everywhere. According to Purina, pugs are often referred to as "multum in parvo" which is latin for "much in little." This is referring to pugs big personalites but their small size, as pugs are very expressive and sensitive, and they can often sense their owners' moods.
